Box-Delivered Firewood Subscriptions: What You Get

Subscription firewood services deliver a specified quantity of kiln-dried firewood on a regular schedule — typically monthly or bi-monthly. The best services specify species, verify moisture content to below 20 percent, and deliver in convenient carrying boxes or bags. Price typically runs $30 to $60 per delivery for 15 to 25 pounds of kiln-dried wood.

For buyers who use wood for occasional campfires, weekend fireplace sessions, or outdoor entertainment rather than primary heating, subscriptions offer a convenient way to always have dry, quality firewood on hand without managing a large inventory. For primary heating needs requiring several cords, subscriptions become expensive relative to local bulk purchasing.

Amazon Bundle Firewood: Quality Without Subscription

Amazon offers kiln-dried firewood bundles from verified brands at consistent quality and price points. Brands like Cutting Edge Firewood specify moisture content, species, and use cases. Delivery to your door eliminates hauling, and Prime eligible products arrive within 2 days for most locations.

For buyers who want quality certification without subscription commitment, Amazon bundles are the best option. They cost slightly more than bulk local purchasing but significantly less than convenience store bundles while providing quality that local markets often cannot guarantee.

When Local Buying Beats Everything Else

For buyers who heat primarily with wood and need 3 to 5 cords per season, local bulk purchasing remains the most economical option. A face cord of well-dried hardwood from a reputable local supplier at $80 to $150 provides far more BTU per dollar than any delivery service or box format.

Find local suppliers by searching your county's Craigslist, farm supply stores, and NextDoor. Ask specifically about species, when the wood was split, and moisture content. A supplier who welcomes these questions and lets you use your moisture meter is a keeper.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about firewood delivery service, updated based on current research and reader feedback.

Are firewood delivery subscriptions worth the cost?

For occasional users burning wood on weekends or for campfires, yes. Subscriptions provide convenience, consistent kiln-dried quality, and eliminate the hassle of sourcing and hauling. For primary heating needs requiring multiple cords per season, local bulk buying is significantly more economical.

What is the cheapest way to buy firewood?

Buying directly from a logger or tree service that clears wood and needs to dispose of it. This gets you the cheapest wood but requires splitting and seasoning over 12 to 24 months. Buying in-season from a local farm or woodlot is the next most economical option. Amazon and subscription services offer convenience but not lowest price.

Does Amazon deliver firewood to rural areas?

Yes, Amazon delivers to most US addresses including rural areas via standard shipping. Some prime 2-day delivery may extend to 3 to 5 days in remote areas. Check delivery dates when ordering. Heavier items like full firewood boxes may incur additional shipping fees for non-Prime members.

How many pounds of firewood do I need for one evening campfire?

A typical 3 to 4 hour campfire burns approximately 8 to 15 pounds of hardwood depending on fire size and weather. A 15-pound box of kiln-dried wood provides one full evening of campfire. If you want a larger fire or colder conditions, plan for 20 to 25 pounds per evening.

Can I set up Amazon Subscribe and Save for firewood?

Some firewood products on Amazon support Subscribe and Save discounts, typically 5 to 15 percent. Search for firewood bundles with the Subscribe and Save option enabled. This works best for buyers who use a consistent quantity on a regular schedule.

What is the best firewood for a fire pit subscription?

Oak bundles provide the best value for regular fire pit use: high BTU, excellent coal bed, and clean burn. Cedar and juniper are popular for their aroma. Hickory is premium for heat and the classic barbecue campfire smell. Most subscription services offer species selection.

How do subscription firewood services compare to local delivery?

Subscription services typically offer kiln-dried certified wood with guaranteed moisture content — something most local delivery services cannot provide. Local delivery is usually cheaper per BTU but quality is less consistent. Subscriptions win on quality; local delivery wins on price per BTU.

Is there a minimum order for firewood delivery?

Amazon has no minimum order for firewood bundles. Subscription services typically start at one bundle per delivery (15 to 25 lbs). Local cord wood delivery typically has a minimum of one face cord. Some local suppliers have minimum delivery quantities of a half cord to make delivery economical.
